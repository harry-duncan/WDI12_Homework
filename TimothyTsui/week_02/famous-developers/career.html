Career highlights

  • In 1978, her design was chosen to build the first Acorn Micro Computer.
  • In 1981, she built the Acorn Proton microcomputer in 5 days, sourcing memory from Hitachi, but the software had bugs so she stayed up all night on the last day fixing them. It was chosen by the BBC and sold to the public to educate them about computers and programming. By the end of it's lifecycle, it sold 1 million units instead of it's targeted 12000
  • In 1983, she began work on the instruction set for one of the first RISC processors, the Acorn RISC Machine (ARM) which is used in 95% of the world's smartphones!
