The Supervillain

As one of the most important figures in computer science, a key player in World War 2, the leader of a top secret decrypting project, and a futurist with exciting ideas for the future, Alan Turing has attracted a large number of portrayals in popular media, including multiple movie appearances. Most portrayals are serious in nature, and his sexuality is a common theme.

A more light-hearted and fun portrayal is given in the comic Atomic Robo, a comic by Brian Clevinger and Scott Wegener. The comic focuses on a range of 20th century scientists, following its robot protagonist throughout the range. Volume 6, The Gost of Station X addresses Turing and his legacy. The comic occurs in the modern day, and details a conspiracy around the disappearance of the shed in which Turing worked (which vanishes overnight, leaving behind a complex collection of cut wires in the basement.

The complete comic is available free online.
